Fostering Change:

Using Data to Understand the Lives of Children in Foster Care

FosterCare data prep notebook using RStudio

The objective of this data project is to analyze the distribution of children in foster care in the United States by race. The data will be collected from various sources such as the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services' Administration for Children and Families, and U.S. Census Data. The project began by cleaning and processing the raw data, which involved removing any duplicates or missing values, standardizing variables, and converting the data into a format suitable for analysis. Next, the data was visualized using maps and charts to illustrate the distribution of children in foster care by race across the United States. A continutation of this project could also use statistical techniques such as regression analysis and hypothesis testing to identify any significant differences in the distribution of children in foster care by race. This analysis will be used to identify any disparities in the foster care system and help policymakers develop targeted interventions to address these issues. Overall, this data project aims to provide insights into the racial distribution of children in foster care in the United States and contribute to efforts to improve the welfare of all children in the foster care system.
